The EXPOCODE is the expedition code, assigned by the CCHDO (sometimes changed to match required syntax from a preliminary EXPOCODE generated by the data originator). A single alphanumeric word, without spaces, commas, or "/" characters (but "_" underscore characters are OK) which is a unique cruise identifier code, is required for every cruise dealt with by the CCHDO.

ExpoCodes created after January 2006 will have the following format:

NODCShipCodeYearMonthDay

Example derivation

Ship NameRoger Revelle (NODC Ship Code: 33RR)
Cruise start dateMarch 29, 2009 (2009-03-29)
ExpoCode33RR20090329

ExpoCodes created before January 2006 will keep their established name, but will also be discoverable by the newer style ExpoCode.

Cruise start date - Official ship's log date of departure. If ship has an emergency return to port then continues the cruise, use original date whether or not stations were occupied before the emergency.

If departure date is unknown, date of first station can be used. Beware of shakedown station complications. Note all relevant information related to this alternate day in the documentation.
